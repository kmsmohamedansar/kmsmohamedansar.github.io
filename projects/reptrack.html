Overview

RepTrack Workout Log is a focused iOS fitness app that simplifies workout tracking and makes strength progression easier to review over time. It was designed and developed end to end with an emphasis on clarity, usability, and a smooth native iOS experience.

Problem

Training logs often feel noisy or overbuilt. In practice, lifters need fast capture in the gym and a simple way to see what they did last time — not another dashboard that competes for attention.

Solution

RepTrack centers on a straightforward session model: pick a date, add exercises, log sets and reps, and keep notes when it matters. The UI stays calm and readable so the product fades into the background while you train.

What I built

  • Designed the workout logging flow for speed and low cognitive load
  • Implemented the interface in SwiftUI with consistent hierarchy and spacing
  • Modeled local workout and exercise data with SwiftData
  • Structured the codebase around maintainable iOS patterns
  • Prepared assets, metadata, and App Store Connect submission for review

Highlights

  • End-to-end ownership from concept through App Store submission
  • Native architecture with SwiftUI and SwiftData
  • UX tuned for repeat daily use, not one-off demos
  • Practical focus on consistency, tracking clarity, and real-world constraints

Key features

  • Workouts with exercises, sets, reps, and weight
  • Historical context for progression decisions
  • Focused session flow with minimal distraction
  • Local-first data model — no account friction for core use
  • Fast paths for common actions during a session

Tech stack

SwiftUI SwiftData iOS Xcode App Store Connect

Why it matters

Shipping a consumer-facing iOS app is as much about product judgment as it is about code: what to leave out, how screens behave under time pressure, and how persistence and navigation stay predictable. RepTrack is my proof point for owning that full loop — not only building features, but delivering something polished enough to submit to Apple.

What’s next

Post-launch iteration will follow real usage: refinements to progression insights, optional quality-of-life shortcuts, and anything that makes logging faster without diluting the calm, focused experience.
