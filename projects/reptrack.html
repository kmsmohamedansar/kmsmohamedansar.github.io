Overview

RepTrack Workout Log is a focused iOS fitness app that simplifies workout tracking and makes strength progression easier to review over time. It was designed and developed end to end with an emphasis on clarity, usability, and a smooth native iOS experience.

Problem

Training logs often feel noisy or overbuilt. Lifters need fast capture in the gym and a simple read on what they did last time, not another dashboard fighting for attention.

Solution

RepTrack uses a straightforward session model: pick a date, add exercises, log sets and reps, notes when they matter. The UI stays calm so the product stays out of the way while you train.

What I built

  • Workout logging flow tuned for speed and low cognitive load
  • SwiftUI interface with consistent hierarchy and spacing
  • Local workout and exercise modeling with SwiftData
  • Codebase structured around maintainable iOS patterns
  • Assets, metadata, and App Store Connect submission for review

Highlights

  • End-to-end ownership from concept through App Store submission
  • Native architecture with SwiftUI and SwiftData
  • UX tuned for repeat daily use, not one-off demos
  • Practical focus on consistency, tracking clarity, and real constraints

Key features

  • Workouts with exercises, sets, reps, and weight
  • Historical context for progression decisions
  • Focused session flow with minimal distraction
  • Local-first data model: no account friction for core use
  • Fast paths for common actions during a session

Tech stack

SwiftUI SwiftData iOS Xcode App Store Connect

Why it matters

Shipping consumer iOS is product judgment as much as code: what to omit, how screens behave under pressure, predictable persistence and navigation. RepTrack is the proof I can own that loop and submit work to Apple’s bar.

What’s next

Post-launch iteration follows real usage: progression insights, quality-of-life shortcuts, anything that speeds logging without diluting the calm, focused experience.
